1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT can speed up the time it takes new products to reach the market. Companies can write product requirement documents by gathering market intelligence from proprietary databases, customers and sales representatives. CAD & CAM software speed up, and reduce the cost of the design and manufacture of products. Collaborative technologies allow global teams to work on different components of a product simultaneously. The long-term cost savings are usually worth the IT investment. IT-based productivity solutions have allowed companies to save on the costs while maintaining and improving product quality and customer service. IT is at the core of operating models which is essential for globalisation, such as telecommuting and outsourcing. A company can outsource most of its noncore functions, such as human resources and finances, to offshore companies and use network technologies to stay in contact with its overseas employees, customers and suppliers.

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

There are lots of methodologies behind digital project management but almost all digital projects will go through the same basic 5-step method: Scoping, Planning, Development, Testing & Deployment, Maintenance. There are also skills required that can be gained through basic work experience and collaboration. Skills such as time management, resource management, project communication management, planning, organising, and delegating can be developed in almost any role. Some skills you might need are: Collaboration, strong communication skills, problem solver, attention to detail, strong organisational skills etc. There are lots of digital project management tools available. Software as a service allow projects to get organised, allows for group communication, share files and assign tasks. Some popular tools include: agile tools, planning software, data analytics solutions, and collaboration platforms. More specifically: G suite, Airtable, Slack, and Trello are all helpful resources to successfully complete a digital project.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

Without a risk assessment of digital projects it is highly likely that they could go wrong. One of the biggest risks is failing to plan. By investing time upfront to address risks, you’ll be better prepared to turn unexpected events into new opportunities, instead putting out fires. Estimating time is important if there is a deadline. It is better to overestimate and make a functioning website than to underestimate and rush it to a lower quality. Some risks are unavoidable which can slow down productivity, this can be from technologies being discontinued to changes in government policy. A budget cut is one of the hardest risks to face as you still have to satisfy the client but have limited resources. Today’s market is competitive as ever. This means clients may sometimes change their vision of how the product or certain features on the website should look like. Even though it’s hard to be prepared for change of requirements, it’s one of the things that need to considered or it could result in a delayed launch and unhappy clients.

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software

Selecting the right IT systems and software is crucial for the rest of the project because it needs to do what you need it to do. To start you need to know your objective. You cannot be successful in searching for software unless you know what you're looking for. Then you should create a comprehensive list of requirements for the system, which is where the research is important. Then you can start searching for applications that fulfil your needs. This should be a long list so that no software is overlooked. Then you start excluding some of the options that don’t compare as well as others. This short list may be down to 5 or 10 different systems. Next you can start trialling your selected software whilst constantly assessing the software against your criteria. After that you should have an idea of which system is the best for what you need doing. The final stage is pricing up not just the software cost but implementation time, support levels, and service availability.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) puts the consumer in the drivers seat and it is the companies responsibility to comply with its regulation or they could face serious fines. If the digital project works with personal data, a special job should be created with the sole purpose of making sure they are in compliance with GDPR. Overall this means companies have to be much more careful when working around marketing and sales activities. This means making sure that whenever your website is asking for personal data, it’s also clearly asking for consent.
